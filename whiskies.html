The history of whiskey:

Whiskey was born in Ireland, and its production quickly spread to Scotland. It is a distilled spirit made from cereal and aged in wooden barrels. The pioneers in distilling it and formalizing the production process were monks, and from them, it passed on to the peasants who set up countless homemade stills.

Currently, Whiskey is the most prized spirit in the liquor market. In addition to being of great quality, the English spread it throughout the world with its arrival in Canada and the United States, leading to the proliferation of numerous distilleries.

Types of whiskeys and their differences:

Whiskey blended scotch:

Imagine that on one side we have a single malt Scotch Whiskey; that is, whiskeys that have been created in a single distillery using water, yeast, and malted barley. On the other side, we have a single grain Scotch Whiskey; meaning it has been created in a single distillery where, in addition to water, other grains like corn, rye, wheat, or barley (it can also be a combination of several) were used. These two blended whiskeys can give life to a blended whiskey, yes! It's that simple. A blended whiskey is the combination of one or more single malt scotches with one or more single grain scotches. This type of whiskey is one of the most popular in liquor stores and your favorite bars. In fact, 80% of the blended whiskey volume exported worldwide comes from Scotland.

Irish Whiskey:

The preparation of Irish Whiskey is made from the mash obtained from cereals. This mixture is usually composed of malted and unmalted barley, along with rye, oats, and wheat. The process is similar to that of Scotch Whiskeys. After selecting the grains, each distillery carries out its own specific blend. They are then washed and soaked (hydrated) to undergo germination, a process in which the starches in the grains are converted into soluble sugars. Next, the grains are milled (except those that need to be malted), and after infusion or mashing, the liquid is fermented for approximately 72 hours. Once the wash is obtained, it undergoes three distillations, after which it is aged in oak casks (previously used to store Sherry).

american whiskey:

Bourbon Whiskey, or bourbon, is a distilled beverage from the Whiskey family that is exclusive to the United States. It is characterized by being slightly aromatic and having a caramel-like flavor. According to US legislation, bourbon must be made primarily from corn, with a concentration between 50% and 70%, with the latter being more common. Other added ingredients may include malted barley, wheat, or rye. There are no legal requirements for a minimum aging period for a product to be called bourbon. However, in order to be labeled as "straight bourbon," it must have been aged for a minimum of two years. Additionally, any bourbon aged less than 4 years must state the specific aging period on the label. As a general rule, bourbon is matured for a period of 5 years in oak barrels, which can be new and/or charred.

BALLANTINE'S FINEST WHISKEY

Ballantine's Finest is a complex, refined, and elegant blended Scotch Whiskey. Its flavor is considered ideal to satisfy the modern style. The characteristic flavors of Miltonduff and Glenburgie malts bring notes of chocolate, red apple, and vanilla to Ballantine's Finest.
